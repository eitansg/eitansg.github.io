Abstract: Research on the Courts of Appeals has been limited by the fact that no annotated database covers the full universe of almost 450,000 published opinions from 1892 to 2025. We create this database, covering all such opinions and use multiple large language model approaches to extend the structure of the Songer U.S. Court of Appeals database to this corpus. Our new database includes summary information about each case (e.g. issue area and litigant types), the ideological direction of the panel's decision (liberal or conservative), and the identity of each judge who heard the case or wrote an opinion. We validate our database against multiple existing sources and find that it matches human coders 85-90\% on summary variables and 80\% of the time on ideological direction. This new database has the potential to greatly increase our understanding of changes in the Courts of Appeals over time; as examples, we replicate and extend existing work on panel effects and ideological decisions across panel types.
